# Managing IT in a Digitized World (MITDW)

This repository contains academic notes, assignments, and learning materials from the course **Managing IT in a Digitized World (MITDW)** in the **MSc Business Information Systems** program at **FHNW – University of Applied Sciences and Arts Northwestern Switzerland**.

The purpose of this repository is to document key concepts, frameworks, and insights learned during the course and organize them in a structured format for academic reference.

---

## Course Overview

Managing IT in a Digitized World focuses on how organizations manage information technology in modern digital environments. The course explores how IT supports business strategy, digital transformation, and organizational innovation.

It examines how digital technologies influence business models, decision-making processes, and operational efficiency within organizations.

---

## Topics Covered

The course covers several important areas related to IT management, including:

- IT governance  
- IT strategy and business alignment  
- Digital transformation in organizations  
- Data-driven organizations  
- Technology and innovation management  
- IT service management frameworks (e.g., ITIL)  
- IT governance frameworks (e.g., COBIT)  
- Digital business models  

---

## Repository Structure

This repository is organized into the following sections:

### Frameworks
Descriptions of important IT management frameworks such as **ITIL** and **COBIT**.

### Case Studies
Analysis of real-world digital transformation examples and IT strategy cases.

### Diagrams
Visual models explaining IT management concepts and digital transformation frameworks.

## Learning Outcomes

Through this course, the following competencies were developed:

- Understanding how IT supports business strategy  
- Analyzing digital transformation initiatives  
- Applying IT governance and management frameworks  
- Evaluating the impact of digital technologies on organizations  
- Understanding how IT resources are managed in modern enterprises
